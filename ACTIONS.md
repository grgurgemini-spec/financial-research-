# Study Findings & Recommended Actions — Path to €4,000/mo Passive Income

*Statistical analysis of 10,000 Monte Carlo paths (25 years, monthly steps) from the **Life & Work Plan** model. Every number sourced from `data/lifeplan/results.json` and `data/opportunities.json`. This is a model showing consequences of assumptions — not a forecast, and not financial advice.*

---

## Executive Summary

**Goal:** Reach €4,000/mo passive income (fat FI, €1.2M at 4% SWR) by ~2045, graduating mid-2027 from Master's, starting from €0 saved and €700/mo expenses.

**Verdict:** Likelier than not (86.5% lifetime probability). Median crossing year: **2045**. Ranges: lucky case 2040, unlucky case beyond 2051.

**Strategic One-Liner:**
> Salary track dominance >> savings discipline >> product success. The choice to pursue remote-EU-level work vs. staying on a local Croatian salary track is worth ±16 years of financial freedom — more than every other lever combined. Everything else (side hustles, product, hours, expenses) is secondary but real.

---

## 1. Key Findings

### **F1. The Salary Track Decision Is Worth ±16 Years**

**Evidence:**
- Local Croatian salary path (€1,500 net start, capped ~€3,500): **+16.0 years** to fat FI median, drops lifetime probability from 86.5% → 41.8%
- Aggressive remote track (€2,800+ start, 8% real growth, €9k cap): **−3.5 years**, fat FI probability 98.3%
- This single decision outweighs all others combined: product, side hours, expenses, market returns

**Finding:** You will reach financial freedom faster and more reliably if you spend your effort landing a remote-EU-level geospatial or data engineering role than any other single action. The local-track path is mathematically viable but takes nearly two decades longer.

---

### **F2. Savings Rate 60–70% Is the Engine**

**Evidence:**
- Baseline scenario median net worth trajectory (in real €):
  - 2030: €99k
  - 2035: €320k
  - 2040: €665k
  - 2045: €1,154k
- This trajectory depends on a sustained savings rate of 60–70% (automatic ETF investing, not heroics)
- Every €300/mo of permanent lifestyle creep costs approximately +1.1 years to fat FI

**Finding:** The plan is not about picking lottery income streams. It's about taking normal earnings, living below your means (€700→€1,100/mo expenses, then +€200 step in 2032), and compounding savings into ETF. Boring and reliable.

---

### **F3. Lean FI Is Near-Certain; It Arrives 8 Years Early**

**Evidence:**
- Lean FI (€450k portfolio, €1,500/mo passive): **99.6% lifetime probability**, median year **2037**, range 2035–2041
- Comfortable FI (€750k, €2,500/mo): 97.4% lifetime, median 2041, range 2037–2046
- Fat FI (€1.2M, €4,000/mo, your target): 86.5% lifetime, median 2045, range 2040–beyond 2051

**Finding:** Lean FI is essentially guaranteed if you follow the plan. It's where part-time consulting can sustain you — think of it as the "work becomes optional" milestone (age ~35 if you're ~24 now). You can take a sabbatical, switch to contract work, or step back to part-time from 2037. Fat FI at 2045 is a coin-flip by that year, 85% by 2050.

---

### **F4. The Product (EO SaaS) Moves the Median Only ~1 Year But Drives the Upside Tail**

**Evidence:**
- No-product scenario (job + freelancing only): fat FI median **2046**, 81.7% lifetime (−1 year vs. baseline)
- Baseline (product succeeds 55% chance): fat FI median **2045**, 86.5% lifetime
- Aggressive scenario (product succeeds 85%): fat FI median **2042**, 98.3% lifetime (−3 years combined with aggressive salary)
- Product certain (p=1.0): median shift **−0.8 years**; product never works (p=0): **+1.0 year**

**Finding:** The product doesn't move the *median* much — it's not the leverage point that the salary track is. But it (1) drives the upside tail significantly (you could hit fat FI by 2042 if both product and salary line up), (2) is your best resume artifact for landing the remote role that matters (F1), and (3) is the only income stream that survives if you ever stop working. Build it strategically, not desperately.

---

### **F5. Market Returns (±2y per 2% Deviation) Are a Lever You Don't Control**

**Evidence:**
- Model assumes: 5% real annual return (inflation-adjusted), 15% volatility
- If returns drop to 3% real: **+1.8 years** to fat FI
- If returns jump to 7% real: ~−0.9 years (not separately modeled, symmetric)

**Finding:** Diversify into a boring global ETF, rebalance annually, and ignore drawdowns and crashes. The model's 10,000 paths already include simulated market crashes; the medians already survived them. Dollar-cost averaging (steady monthly ETF buy) is your friend. Do not try to time the market or pick sectors.

---

### **F6. Expenses Are Second-Order (But Permanent Creep Compounds)**

**Evidence:**
- Current assumed baseline: €700/mo (student) → €1,100/mo (independent, 2027) → €1,300/mo (2032 step) → +1.5%/yr
- Expenses −€200/mo (lower trajectory): **−0.7 years** to fat FI
- Expenses +€300/mo (lifestyle creep): **+1.1 years** to fat FI
- Each additional €300/mo of *permanent* spending costs ~+1 year

**Finding:** Don't obsess over monthly optimizations — €20 saved on coffee is noise. But pre-budget all lifestyle increases (moving to own apartment, car, family). Every permanent €300/mo addition is a known cost in years. The model already accounts for inflation; spending inflation (wanting more) is on you.

---

### **F7. Side-Hustle Hours Now Matter Mostly as a Bridge and Skill Ramp, Not Income**

**Evidence:**
- Fewer side hours now (cutting from 25 h/wk to 15 h/wk): **+1.0 year** to fat FI
- Platform income (Phase 1): modeled €200–400/mo, high variance, but immediate cash
- Freelance income (Phase 1): ramped over 6 months to €1,300/mo baseline, skill-dependent

**Finding:** The 25 h/wk available now is valuable because (1) it's available *now*, before you have a salary, (2) it de-risks the transition to Phase 2 (you're already freelancing and have Upwork reviews), and (3) it teaches you pricing, client interaction, scope creep — skills that compound. It's not the €800/mo that's magic; it's the *having done it* that gets you hired for the €2,300/mo salary in Phase 2.

---

### **F8. The First 90 Days (This Year) Are the Highest-Leverage Learning Window**

**Evidence:**
- Top 4 income opportunities (from research, by score):
  1. Freelance automation & scripting (7.09): Upwork gigs, Claude Code leverage, €300–4,500/mo range
  2. Etsy/Gumroad digital products (7.01): 80% automation, €100–3,000/mo, takes 3–6 months
  3. Local websites & tools (6.91): €300–2,500/mo, €60 upfront, no platform fees
  4. GIS/Earth Engine/QGIS freelancing (6.85): €300–3,500/mo, matches your geodesy background perfectly
- All four are portfolio-builders that lead to Phase 2 jobs

**Finding:** Week 1–12 (this quarter) is when you create the assets that carry you through the degree and into the job market. Upwork profile with 5+ reviews, 3 GEE demos, one automated product improvement, and platform account activity. These assets are *cumulative and never wasted* — they work for you in 2027 job applications.

## 6. Why These Actions, In This Order

**Salary track (A1, A2, A11) comes first** because it's the 16-year lever. Everything else is secondary.

**The first 90 days (A2, A3, A5) come first** because they build the portfolio and reviews that make A1 possible. You can't land a remote job without GitHub proof and freelance reviews.

**Savings automation (A4, A8) comes at Phase 2** start (when salary arrives) because Phase 1 cash is too variable to automate. But set it up mentally now.

**The product (A5, A6) is strategic, not desperate.** You ship one improvement by end-2026, then measure coldly. If it's not clicking, kill it and redirect energy. The resume value is in "I built and shipped an automated EO product," not "I ground on this 18 months unpaid."

**Expenses (A7) are monitored, not obsessed over.** The plan works at €1,100–€1,300/mo. Each permanent €300 increase costs ~1 year. That's the math; spend consciously, not accidentally.

## 7. Methodology & Reproduction

### Data Sources
- **Income distributions**: `data/opportunities.json` (25 scored opportunities, research from July 2026)
- **Simulation engine**: `lifeplan/model.py` (seeded numpy, SEED=42, N_PATHS=10,000, 300 monthly steps)
- **Career phases**: anchored to real dates (2026-08 graduation → 2027-07 Phase 2 start → 2029 Phase 3 → 2032+ Phase 4)
- **Salary ranges**: researched from Space-Careers, GEO CAREERS, EO-jobs, adjusted for EU/Croatian nets
- **Tax assumptions**: Croatian paušalni obrt 12% flat (druga djelatnost, no monthly contributions), 2-year ETF holding exemption
- **Market assumptions**: 5% real annual return, 15% volatility, geometric Brownian motion

### Reproduction
```bash
# Re-run the model with current assumptions:
python3 lifeplan/model.py

# Regenerate all charts:
python3 lifeplan/charts.py

# Spot-check results.json
python3 -c "import json; R=json.load(open('data/lifeplan/results.json')); print('Fat FI median:', R['scenarios']['baseline']['fi']['fat (4k/mo)']['median_year'])"
```

### Caveat: Not a Forecast
A model shows **consequences of assumptions**, not a prophecy. The income ranges come from published research that skews optimistic (self-reported blog posts, Upwork profiles). The model anchors on conservative *midpoints*, but true uncertainty is wider. Simulated in real (today's) EUR, so nominal figures will be larger. FI = first touch of threshold; sequence-of-returns risk around retirement is not separately modeled.

**Croatian tax rules (paušalni obrt 12%, €60k limit, ETF 2-year exemption) are 2026 rules and will change over 25 years.** Verify with an accountant; this is research, not tax or financial advice.
